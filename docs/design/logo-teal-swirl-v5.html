Teal Open Swirl — Refinements

Based on the teal open variant. More rippled, more open curves, each arc has different gap positions and lengths. Softer rounded typefaces. Eight variations exploring different moods within the same palette.

V1. Staggered Ripple

Each arc opens at a different position (top-right, bottom-left, right). Curves have slight wobble. Quicksand typeface — round, soft, friendly.

V3. Water Ripple

Concentric waves expanding from a center drop — like when the chasen touches the surface. Each ripple opens in a different direction. Tiny splash dots above. Plus Jakarta Sans — clean and contemporary.

V4. Loose Swoosh

Three independent swooshes at different angles — not concentric, more like brush strokes circling loosely around a center. Very airy, lots of breathing room. DM Sans — geometric and clean with soft weight.

V5. Four Rings

Four thinner rings instead of three — more texture, more ripple feel. Each gap points in a completely different clock direction (2, 7, 11, 5). Quicksand bold with letter-spacing for a calmer, spaced-out feel.

V5. Dark

V6. Taper

Each ring has a different stroke weight — outer is thick (3.5), middle is medium (2.2), inner is thin (1.5). Creates a sense of depth and dissipation, like ripples fading outward. Nunito medium weight.

V6. Dark

Tapered rings on dark. The weight difference creates depth.
