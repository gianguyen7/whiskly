A. Chasen Tines with Foam Bubbles

A recognizable chasen shape — the splayed bamboo tines fanning upward from a bound center — with small circles (microfoam bubbles) floating around the top. The tines have varying thickness to show the characteristic bamboo splits. Clean and balanced.

B. Top-Down Bowl View — Foam Surface with Whisk Pattern

Looking down into a matcha bowl from above — a circle represents the chawan, inside which you see the spiral pattern left by the chasen in the foam, with scattered microfoam bubbles. The view most matcha drinkers recognize: that moment you look down at a freshly whisked bowl.

C. Chasen Silhouette in Bowl — Side View

A minimal side-profile of a chasen resting in a matcha bowl. The bowl is a gentle U-curve, the whisk a rounded shape with visible tine lines, and foam bubbles rise from the surface. The most literal representation — immediately readable as "matcha whisk in bowl."

D. Minimal Chasen — Rounded Tines with Foam Dots

The most refined version. Just the business end of the chasen: 5 rounded tines with gentle outward curves (like fingers spreading), and 3-4 foam dots nestled between the tines. No handle, no bowl — maximum reduction while staying recognizable. Works beautifully as a compact app icon.
