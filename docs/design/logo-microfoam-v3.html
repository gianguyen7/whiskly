Microfoam Only — Logo Exploration v3

Pure microfoam. No chasen, no bowl — just the bubbles themselves. The foam on a freshly whisked matcha is instantly recognizable to anyone who drinks it. These concepts explore different ways to capture that satisfying, organic cluster of bubbles as a logo mark.

E. Organic Bubble Cluster

A tight, organic arrangement of circles in varying sizes — like looking at real microfoam up close. The bubbles pack together naturally with small gaps between them. Feels alive, tactile, slightly imperfect. The overall silhouette reads as roughly circular without being a perfect circle.

F. Bowl Ring with Foam Fill

A thin circle (the bowl rim) with an interior filled by a cluster of packed microfoam bubbles. The container gives it structure and makes it app-icon-ready, while the foam inside is the star. The bubbles are tightly packed with small light highlights — like a macro photo of matcha foam.

G. Three Bubbles — Ultra Minimal

Maximum restraint: just 3 overlapping circles of different sizes. Suggests foam without spelling it out. The asymmetric arrangement keeps it from feeling like a generic diagram. Works at every size, extremely memorable, almost logo-as-punctuation. The "W" dot.
